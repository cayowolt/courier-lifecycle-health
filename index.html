📐 Health Score Methodology

How is the Health Score calculated? ▼
Health Score (0–100) = equal 25% weight across four stages:

Onboarding (25%) — conversion-only: % of applicants reaching "ready" status. Speed is shown as a contextual flag but does not penalise the score — long timelines often reflect legal/ops constraints, not CRM performance.

FTD Activation (25%) — % of applicants who deliver (60% weight) + % still active 7 days after FTD (40% weight).

Nurture (25%) — 30-day retention after FTD (50%) + avg active hours/30d (30%) + CRM conversion rate (20%).

Retention (25%) — monthly churn rate (70%) + new-courier dependency share (30%), averaged over 6 months.

Grades: 75+ = Healthy · 50–74 = At Risk · <50 = Critical.
Why was speed removed from the Onboarding score? ▼
The previous formula penalised countries like Austria (avg 940 days to FTD) and Iceland (936 days) — timelines driven primarily by document verification regulations and legal requirements that CRM has no power to change. Penalising the CRM team's score for regulatory friction gives a misleading picture of CRM performance.

Speed is now shown as a contextual warning flag (⚠ or ℹ) in the country detail panel, so ops and legal teams can still track it — but it doesn't pull down the health score. The onboarding score now purely reflects whether the application-to-ready funnel is converting.
How does the Country Compare mode work? ▼
In the Countries tab, click one country card to open its diagnosis. Then click a second card — a comparison panel appears showing both countries' radar charts side by side, a metric diff table (which country leads on each measure), and the treatment recommendation for whichever country scores lower. Click the ✕ on either card to exit compare mode.
How is the CRM Priority Queue ranked? ▼
urgency = (100 − health_score) × ln(total_applicants)

Larger unhealthy markets rank higher — fixing a fleet of 20,000 applicants with health=30 delivers more absolute impact than fixing 300 applicants with health=20.

📊 Metric Definitions

Onboarding metrics ▼
% ready-to-deliver: % of applicants in the 90-day cohort who completed all verification and reached "ready" status. Source: COURIER_ONBOARDING_COHORT.IS_READY.

Avg days app → FTD: calendar days from application to first delivery, averaged across the cohort. Context flag only — not in score. Source: COURIER_ONBOARDING_COHORT.FROM_APPLICATION_TO_1_DELIVERY.
FTD Activation metrics ▼
FTD conversion rate: % of all applicants who completed ≥1 delivery. Measured against total pool (not just ready couriers). Source: HAS_DELIVERED.

Active 7d after FTD: % of FTD couriers who delivered again within 7 days. Strongest predictor of long-term retention. Source: CONVERSION_7_DAYS.
Nurture & Retention metrics ▼
Active 30d after FTD: % of FTD couriers still delivering 30 days later. Source: CONVERSION_30_DAYS.

Avg active hrs/30d: mean on-duty hours per courier per 30 days. Source: COURIER.CRM_EVENTS joined with COURIER_PARTNER_METRICS.

Monthly churn rate: net % of active couriers who lapsed each month, 6-month average. Source: CAPITAL_OKR_AGGREGATED_COURIER_CHURN (data to Aug 2025).
Campaign metrics ▼
Open rate: % of sends that were opened. Email benchmark: 35–55%. Push benchmark: 15–30%.

Click rate: % of total sends that resulted in a click (not % of opens).

Conversion rate: % of sends where the recipient completed a target action (shift taken, delivery started, etc.).

Unsubscribe rate: % of sends that resulted in an unsubscribe. Rates above 0.4% signal messaging fatigue or irrelevance.

Note: Campaign data currently uses representative sample data based on observed Intercom patterns. Live data requires reconnecting to Iterable via Snowflake (DATASHARE_INBOUND_ITERABLE.ORG_2249).

⚠️ Data Limitations

Known limitations ▼
90-day cohort window: Onboarding metrics only count couriers who applied in the last 90 days. Countries with legally long onboarding (e.g. Austria, Iceland) have many in-progress applications not yet reflected in pct_ready or FTD rate.

Churn data recency: CAPITAL_OKR_AGGREGATED_COURIER_CHURN data runs to August 2025. Countries without recent data default to a neutral Retention score of 50.

Campaign data: The Campaigns tab currently uses representative sample data. Live campaign performance requires re-querying Iterable via DATASHARE_INBOUND_ITERABLE.ORG_2249 — the schema is mapped and queries are ready.

CRM attribution: The Nurture score's CRM conversion component is weak — Intercom message titles lack a lifecycle_stage field. Mapping Iterable campaign metadata will improve this signal significantly.

Equal stage weighting: All four stages carry 25%. Consider adjusting weights to reflect quarterly priorities.
