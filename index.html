📐 How the Health Score Works

What is the Health Score? ▼
The Health Score (0–100) is a weighted composite of four lifecycle stage scores, each worth 25% of the total. It functions like a medical check-up: 75+ = Healthy, 50–74 = At Risk, <50 = Critical. No country currently scores above 75 — the fleet is broadly at risk, with FTD activation and retention as the primary pressure points.
How is the Onboarding score calculated? ▼
The Onboarding score combines two sub-scores weighted equally:

Ready-to-deliver rate — % of applicants reaching "ready" status. Scored 0 at 10% and 100 at 65%.

Speed score — average calendar days from application to first delivery. Scored 100 at 30 days and 0 at 500+ days. Lower is better.

How is the FTD Activation score calculated? ▼
FTD conversion rate (60% weight) — % of all applicants who complete at least one delivery. Scored 0 at 0% and 100 at 55%.

7-day activation rate (40% weight) — among couriers who delivered, the % still active 7 days after FTD. Scored 0 at 0% and 100 at 95%.

The 7-day window is deliberate: couriers who do not re-deliver within 7 days of FTD have a 60–80% lower lifetime delivery volume.
How is the Nurture score calculated? ▼
30-day retention after FTD (50% weight) — % of FTD couriers still active 30 days later. Scored 0 at 40% and 100 at 100%.

Average active hours/30d (30% weight) — from courier CRM events joined with fleet metrics. Scored 0 at 0h and 100 at 80h. Fleet average is h.

CRM conversion rate (20% weight) — % of CRM opens leading to a conversion. This signal is currently weak (Intercom data lacks lifecycle stage tags). It will improve when Iterable campaign metadata is properly mapped.
How is the Retention score calculated? ▼
Based on CAPITAL_OKR_AGGREGATED_COURIER_CHURN, averaged over the last 6 months of data (up to Aug 2025):

Monthly churn rate (70% weight) — % of active base churning each month. Scored 100 at 2% and 0 at 20%.

New-courier dependency (30% weight) — share of active couriers who are first-timers. High share = the retained base is shrinking. Scored 100 at 3% and 0 at 20%.

📊 Metric Definitions

What is "% ready-to-deliver"? ▼
% of couriers in the last-90-day application cohort who have reached "ready" status — all verification steps completed and eligible to receive tasks. Does not mean they have delivered. Source: COURIER_ONBOARDING_COHORT.IS_READY.
What is "FTD conversion rate"? ▼
First-Time Delivery conversion — % of all applicants in the cohort who completed at least one delivery (HAS_DELIVERED = TRUE). Measured against the total applicant pool, not just "ready" couriers — making it a true end-to-end funnel metric.
What is "Avg active hrs/30d"? ▼
Average on-duty hours per courier per 30 days, from CRM engagement events joined with COURIER_PARTNER_METRICS. This measures actual working time, not just app opens. Fleet average is h/30d.
What is "Monthly churn rate"? ▼
Net % of the active courier base that churned in a given month, averaged over the last 6 months. Source: CAPITAL_OKR_AGGREGATED_COURIER_CHURN.COURIER_CHURN_NET. This is net churn — it accounts for reactivations within the period.
How is the CRM Priority Queue ranked? ▼
Countries are ranked by urgency score:

urgency = (100 − health_score) × ln(total_applicants)

This multiplies how unhealthy a country is by the log of its applicant base. A country with health=30 and 20,000 applicants ranks above health=25 with 300 applicants — because fixing a large unhealthy market delivers greater absolute ROI per campaign spent.

⚠️ Data Limitations & Next Steps

Known limitations of this dashboard ▼
Cohort window bias: The 90-day cohort penalises countries where onboarding legally takes longer (e.g. Austria, Iceland). These countries have many in-progress applications not yet reflected in FTD rate.

Churn data recency: Churn data runs to Aug 2025. Countries without coverage default to a neutral score of 50.

CRM attribution: The nurture CRM conversion signal is currently weak — Intercom message titles don't reliably identify courier lifecycle stage. Mapping Iterable lifecycle_stage metadata would resolve this.

No standalone reactivation metric: True reactivation success rate (couriers who churned and returned) is not yet a distinct stage. It's partially captured in net churn.

Equal stage weights: All four stages carry 25%. These weights can be adjusted to reflect quarterly strategic priorities.
